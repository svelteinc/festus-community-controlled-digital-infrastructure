# FDIA/FCIC Charter & Bylaws – Skeleton (Draft v1)

> **Note:** This is a high-level skeleton to help cities and counsel structure a
> charter/bylaws package for a Festus-style community-controlled digital
> infrastructure authority (FDIA/FCIC). It is not legal advice.

## Article I – Name, Purpose, and Powers
- **Section 1.1 – Name**
  - Festus Digital Infrastructure Authority (FDIA) / Festus Community
    Infrastructure Corporation (FCIC).
- **Section 1.2 – Purpose**
  - Public-purpose mission, including community-controlled digital
    infrastructure and long-term stewardship of covenants.
- **Section 1.3 – Powers**
  - Authority to enter into ground leases, enforce covenants, receive and
    allocate revenues, and enter into financing arrangements consistent with
    city and state law.

## Article II – Board Composition and Appointment
- **Section 2.1 – Board Size**
  - 7 members as described in the governance document (city appointees, local
    institution, citizen seats, technical expert, at-large).
- **Section 2.2 – Appointment Process**
  - How each seat is nominated and confirmed.
- **Section 2.3 – Term Lengths and Limits**
  - Staggered terms, reappointment rules, and term limits.

## Article III – Meetings and Procedures
- **Section 3.1 – Regular and Special Meetings**
  - Frequency, notice requirements, and public access.
- **Section 3.2 – Quorum**
  - Definition of quorum (e.g., 4 of 7 members) and rules for remote
    participation.
- **Section 3.3 – Voting**
  - Simple majority rules for routine matters.
- **Section 3.4 – Reserved Matters (Supermajority)**
  - Reference to decisions requiring 5/7 supermajority and citizen support
    (e.g., land sale, covenant changes, major expansions, revenue allocation
    changes).

## Article IV – Conflict of Interest and Ethics
- **Section 4.1 – Conflict of Interest Policy**
  - Disclosure obligations and recusal rules.
- **Section 4.2 – Prohibited Conduct**
  - Self-dealing, misuse of confidential information, and related-party
    transactions without proper review.

## Article V – Committees and Advisory Bodies
- **Section 5.1 – Standing Committees**
  - Finance/audit, community engagement, and technical advisory committees.
- **Section 5.2 – Community Advisory Council**
  - Structure for ongoing resident and stakeholder input.

## Article VI – Financial Management
- **Section 6.1 – Budgeting and Reporting**
  - Annual budget approval, public reporting, and transparency requirements.
- **Section 6.2 – Revenue Allocation**
  - Framework for allocating ground rent and fees (e.g., reserves, community
    benefit funds, operating costs).
- **Section 6.3 – Borrowing and Guarantees**
  - Rules for issuing bonds or other obligations and clear prohibition on
    guaranteeing private operator debt without explicit public approval.

## Article VII – Covenants, Leases, and Operator Agreements
- **Section 7.1 – Ground Lease Principles**
  - Term length, permitted uses, and performance standards.
- **Section 7.2 – Covenants and Community Protections**
  - Noise, water, environmental, and expansion caps aligned with the governance
    document.
- **Section 7.3 – Enforcement and Remedies**
  - Mechanisms for addressing breaches, including cure periods and
    step-in/termination rights.

## Article VIII – Amendments and Dissolution
- **Section 8.1 – Amendments**
  - Procedures and voting thresholds (including reserved matter protections) to
    amend charter/bylaws.
- **Section 8.2 – Dissolution**
  - Conditions and process for winding down the authority, including how assets
    and long-term covenants are handled.

## Article IX – Miscellaneous
- **Section 9.1 – Governing Law**
  - Applicable state and local law.
- **Section 9.2 – Severability**
  - Standard severability clause.
- **Section 9.3 – Effective Date**
  - When the charter/bylaws come into force.
